Looking for pickleball leagues or tournaments near you in Montgomery County, DC, or Northern Virginia? Dill Dinkers Rockville and North Bethesda run the most active competitive pickleball programs in the area — and they're open to all skill levels.

## Pickleball Leagues at Dill Dinkers

The [Dill Dinkers league program](/programs/leagues) runs 6-week seasons with DUPR-based brackets, meaning you'll always play against teams at your level. The Spring 2026 season runs March 2 through April 27.

### What Makes These Leagues Different

- **DUPR brackets** — Skill-based matchmaking so games are competitive and fair
- **Indoor courts** — No weather cancellations, consistent playing conditions
- **Two locations** — 10 different league slots across Rockville and North Bethesda
- **Playoff week** — Top teams compete in a double elimination bracket in Week 7
- **Community** — Same partner all season builds teamwork and connection

### Skill Levels Available

Brackets range from 2.5 DUPR to 4.1+, including a dedicated Women's league. Whether you're a newer player looking for your first league experience or a competitive 4.0+ player, there's a bracket for you.

### How to Join

1. Create a free [DUPR account](https://www.dupr.com) if you don't have one
2. Find a partner (or use the [Partner Finder form](https://sammorrispb.notion.site/277fa3ac27dc800b9b0adeaa30dfe34b?pvs=105))
3. Register through CourtReserve for your preferred bracket and location
4. Check the full [league schedule and rules](/programs/leagues)

## Link & Dink Tournament Series

The [Link & Dink Tournament Series](https://tournamentwebsite.vercel.app/) is a community-first tournament program with round robin pools, bracket play, medals, and raffles. Tournaments run monthly at both Rockville and North Bethesda.

### Tournament Format

- **Round robin pools** — Everyone plays multiple games (5+ guaranteed)
- **Playoff bracket** — Top teams advance to elimination rounds
- **Medal points leaderboard** — Gold (3), Silver (2), Bronze (1) tracked across events
- **Duration** — About 3 hours per tournament
- **DUPR brackets** — 3.0–3.5, 3.5–4.0, 4.0–4.5, Women's, and Seniors 50+ divisions

### Why Players Love Link & Dink Tournaments

The tournaments are designed to be competitive but community-focused. Self-officiated with generous line calls, there's a strong emphasis on connection alongside competition. Plus, the JOOLA-sponsored series includes raffles and prizes.

Players come from across Montgomery County, Washington DC, Silver Spring, Bethesda, Potomac, Gaithersburg, and Northern Virginia to compete.

## Not Ready for Leagues Yet?

If competitive play feels like a big step, start with [Coached Open Play](/programs/coached-open-play) to build skills with real-time coaching, or drop into [Open Play](/programs/open-play) to meet other players at your level. Many league players started exactly this way.

When you're ready, the league and tournament community is welcoming and supportive — competition and connection go hand in hand.
